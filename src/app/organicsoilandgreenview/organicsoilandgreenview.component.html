Organic Soil & Green View

Organic Soil

The type of soil we used remarkably matters if we are farmers, gardeners, or organic product lovers. Healthy soil is the foundation of the food system because it produces healthy crops that, in turn, nourish people. Maintaining healthy soil requires care and effort because farming is not benign. Plants obtain nutrients from natural sources such as organic matter and minerals. Organic soil is formed by decomposing plant and animal components to create a nutrient and mineral-rich mini-ecosystem with microorganisms that feed and breathe life back into the soil. Its scientific definition is "Of, relating to, or derived from living matter." It provides nutrients and habitat to organisms living in the soil; organic matter also binds soil particles into aggregates and improves the soil's water-holding ability.

The preservation of soil organic matter levels and the optimization of nutrient cycling are essential to the maintained productivity of agricultural operations. Crop production worldwide has usually appeared in decline in soil organic matter levels and, consequently, soil fertility decline. However, recovery is possible through unused lands, mixed crop-livestock and agroforestry systems, and crop rotations. Cover crops, intercropping, and crop rotations can also increase biodiversity below the soil surface and aboveground. This diversity is essential to maintaining a well-functioning and durable environmental system. Triratna Foundation, Inc. will play the role and support organic farming in the diaspora.

Green View

Green View (Green plant) removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, generates oxygen, stabilizes the soil, and gives life to wildlife. It is also a good source of food besides being pleasing to the eye and contributes to people's mental, physical and emotional wellbeing. Besides, they provide us with the supplies for tools and shelter. Indoor plants improve the air quality of interior spaces, and the levels of relative humidity rise as more plants are added, thereby improving human health and comfort.

The plants absorb and filter out dirt and pollutants and produce a cleaner air quality. The green view supports reducing nitrate that enters surface water runoff by keeping harmful pollutants away from waterways. Living adjacent to greenery enhances our mental health, positively impacts our cognitive and psychological health, and reduces stress. The first noticeable benefit of planting trees is they build homes for wildlife. Hundreds of critters, insects, and birds use trees as shelter, food sources, and homes. Planting trees also contribute to growing the diversity of flora and fauna in the area. Therefore, Triratna Foundation, Inc. will partner with any government, organization, or individuals to support the green view movement.
