Fresh Air & Clean Water

Low-resource countries are disproportionally overwhelmed by chronic lung disease (CLD) due to early childhood disadvantages and indoor/outdoor air pollution. However, data on the socioeconomic impact of respiratory infections in these settings are mostly lacking. Therefore, we aimed to estimate the chronic lung disease-related socioeconomic burden in diverse low-resource backgrounds worldwide. To inform government and health policies worldwide, we focused on work productivity and activity impairment and its modifiable clinical and environmental risk factors (Brakema et al., 2019). The severity of breathlessness, smoking, and solid fuel use are significant modifiable risk factors for impairment. We want to increase awareness on the impact of CLD and the risk factors, preventive actions regarding tobacco and fuel use, and enhance clinical management of CLD in low-resource settings by healthcare workers, policymakers, patients, and governments (Brakema et al., 2019).

The 17th Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) of the United Nations were constructed to address global crises including poverty, inequality, climate, environmental degradation, peace, and justice. The SDG sees that too many people still lack access to safe water supplies and sanitation facilities (Goss., 2021). Our projects provide the UN, the governments, and policymakers an opportunity to grow as global citizens through increasing access to clean water and sanitation and developing their effectiveness. We'll communicate, convince, and share the importance of clean water.
